# Substack Formatting Guide

## Key features to use

**Subtitle field:** Shows in email previews and feed. Make it complement the headline, not repeat it. Headline = hook, subtitle = context.

**Email preview:** The first 2-3 sentences appear in the email preview and feed snippet. Front-load the interesting stuff. If your opening is boring, nobody opens the email.

**Headers (H2):** Break sections every 200-400 words. Readers scan on phones and decide what to read closely.

**Bold key phrases:** Not whole sentences. Just the phrase that carries the point.

**Code blocks:** Render well on Substack. Keep snippets to 5-15 lines. Link to the full file/repo for the complete picture.

**Images:** Break up text and show the real product. Caption them conversationally: "This is what the new search looks like (yes, the icon is still ugly, I know)."

## Substack-specific strengths

**Comments section:** End with a question or invitation. "Has anyone else dealt with this? What worked for you?" Substack's community features are its differentiator.

**Thread feature:** If your post is part of a series, mention previous posts and link to them.

**Notes:** Consider cross-posting a teaser to Substack Notes to drive traffic to the full post.

## What to avoid on Substack

- Don't embed too many external links early in the post (email clients sometimes flag this)
- Don't use complex HTML. Substack's editor is markdown-friendly but limited
- Don't put important content in images only (email clients may not load them)
- Don't use tables heavily (they render poorly on mobile email clients)

## Output structure for Substack

```markdown
# Headline Here

*Subtitle that complements the headline*

[Body with H2 sections, bold phrases, code blocks]

---

**Alternative headlines:**
1. Option A
2. Option B
3. Option C

**Suggested Substack tags:** tag1, tag2, tag3
```
